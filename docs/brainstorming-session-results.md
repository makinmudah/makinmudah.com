## Executive Summary

**Session Goals:** Wide-ranging ideation to create a Nuxt-based, SEO-optimized landing page that is simple yet information-rich and persuasive. Target audiences include small business owners with no IT knowledge and students seeking IT learning/mentoring.

## Technique Sessions

### 1. Role Playing - 30 minutes

**Description:** Explored user perspectives by embodying two key personas to understand their needs, fears, and decision-making criteria.

#### Ideas Generated:

**Persona 1: Small Business Owner (Zero IT Knowledge)**

1. Core need: Easy to use, no installation, accessible anywhere (web-based/SaaS)
2. Primary fear: Getting locked into expensive contracts
3. Value messaging: Affordable solutions, simple for non-experts
4. Hero headline: "Hidup makin mudah dengan Makin Mudah"
5. Pricing message: "Bayar Seperlumu, Bayar Semaumu"
6. CTA language: "Intip Yuk" (casual, friendly, exploratory tone)
7. Trust requirement: Proof that solutions are actually simple

**Persona 2: Student (Learning IT Skills)**

8. Core need: Easy learning approach with step-by-step tutorials and hands-on projects
9. Critical requirement: Mentor supervision and guidance
10. Support system: Media platform to share difficulties and get help
11. Primary fear: Too expensive for student budget
12. Messaging: "You'll never learn alone" (or Indonesian: "Belajar Bareng, Bukan Sendirian")
13. CTA language: "Mulai dulu" (encouraging, low-pressure)
14. Proof needed: Evidence of affordable mentoring and community support

#### Insights Discovered:

- Two distinct user journeys require different messaging and CTAs
- Both personas share fear of high costs but for different reasons (contracts vs budget)
- Trust-building is critical but manifests differently (simplicity proof vs mentorship availability)
- Language tone must be conversational and non-technical for both audiences

#### Notable Connections:

- Both personas need "accessible anywhere" solutions (alignment opportunity)
- Unified messaging around affordability addresses both audiences' fears
- Mentorship differentiates Makin Mudah from typical SaaS competitors

### Insights & Learnings

- **Dual-audience doesn't mean dual landing pages:** Unified messaging in middle sections creates efficiency while strategic CTAs provide personalization

- **Indonesian cultural tone matters:** Casual, friendly language ("Intip Yuk", "Mulai dulu") reduces intimidation and builds approachability - this isn't just translation, it's cultural adaptation

- **Fear-driven decision making:** Both personas' primary blocker is fear (expensive contracts, can't afford it) - addressing fear directly is more important than feature listing

- **Progressive disclosure works:** Hero gives choice, middle unifies with value, FAQ addresses doubts, final CTA converts - respects user's journey rather than forcing early commitment

- **Visual proof > text claims:** Combining solutions with demos (GIFs, payment visuals) significantly strengthens credibility for non-technical audiences

- **Smart UX requires simple foundations first:** Build static, clear landing page before adding adaptive elements like smart floating CTAs

## Reflection & Follow-up

### What Worked Well

- **Persona-first approach** - Starting with role playing created empathy and informed all subsequent decisions
- **Journey mapping provided clarity** - Walking through the scroll experience section-by-section prevented abstract discussions
- **SCAMPER unlocked creativity** - Systematic prompts generated ideas we wouldn't have reached through freeform brainstorming
- **Indonesian language focus** - Keeping cultural context front-and-center ensured authentic, not translated, messaging
- **Balancing ambition with pragmatism** - Identified both immediate implementations and future innovations

### Areas for Further Exploration

- **Pricing model details:** How exactly does "Bayar Seperlumu, Bayar Semaumu" work? Subscription tiers? Pay-per-use? Need pricing strategy session
- **Catalogue page structure:** Netflix-style cards identified, but need to brainstorm specific applications and mentoring service offerings
- **SEO content strategy:** Beyond technical SEO, what blog/content will drive organic traffic? Keyword research needed
- **Student onboarding flow:** After "Mulai dulu" CTA, what's the experience? Mentorship matching process needs design
- **Business application showcase:** What apps are "coming soon"? Need product roadmap clarity to inform landing page promises
- **Conversion tracking:** What metrics define success? Need analytics strategy for testing and iteration

### Recommended Follow-up Techniques

- **Morphological Analysis:** For pricing model design - list all parameters (billing frequency, feature tiers, user limits) and explore combinations
- **Assumption Reversal:** Challenge assumption that single landing page serves both audiences - what if separate pages performed better?
- **Question Storming:** Generate 50+ questions about the Makin Mudah offering to uncover gaps in product definition
- **Five Whys:** Dig deeper into "why mentorship?" to clarify unique value proposition vs competitors

### Questions That Emerged

- What happens after a user clicks "Intip Yuk" vs "Mulai dulu"? What are the distinct experiences?
- How do we prove "simple even for non-experts" before users sign up? Demo videos? Interactive preview?
- What's the mentor vetting/matching process? How does that get communicated on the landing page?
- Should there be a "coming soon" indicator for applications under development?
- What's the minimum viable catalogue (apps + services) needed before launching the landing page?
- How does pricing actually work? Is it per-application? Per-user? Monthly subscription?
- What competitors exist in Indonesian market? How do we differentiate in messaging?
- Should there be language toggle (Indonesian/English) or Indonesian-only?
